Maria Sibylla Merian

pioneering artist of flora and fauna

Chronology

  • 1647 Maria Sibylla Merian is born on April 2 in Frankfurt, Germany.
  • 1660 At the age of 13, she begins her formal art training under her father, Jacob Merian, who was a well-known engraver.
  • 1665 Merian marries Johann Andreas Graff, a painter and engraver, which provides her with an environment conducive to her artistic pursuits.
  • 1679 Following her divorce, she moves to Nuremberg, where she continues to develop her skills and gain recognition for her work in natural history.
  • 1690 She moves to Amsterdam, where she becomes involved with a group of naturalists and continues her studies of entomology and botany.
  • 1701 Merian embarks on a significant journey to the Dutch colony of Surinam in South America to study and document the local flora and fauna.
  • 1705 After returning to Europe, she publishes "Metamorphosis Insectorum Surinamensium," a groundbreaking work that includes detailed illustrations of Surinam's insects and plants.
  • 1717 Maria Sibylla Merian passes away on January 13 in Amsterdam, Netherlands, leaving behind a legacy as a pioneering scientist and artist in the study of natural history.

Legacy

Merian's work is celebrated for its scientific accuracy and artistic quality, significantly influencing the fields of entomology and botany. She is regarded as one of the first female scientists and naturalists.

“Merian combined art and science in a way that was revolutionary for her time, providing detailed studies of insects that were not just scientific but also beautiful.”

-- Ruth Padel,"The Poet and the Naturalist," 2012
